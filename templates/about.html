3. Socially Challenged Students

Additionally, in most school environments, there are some students who face difficulties in mixing with others. This could be due to their introverted natures, physical or mental challenges, different ethnicities, or simply because they may be new to the school.

These students feel socially isolated and may start disliking the school environment, or feel lesser than their peers. In extreme cases, they may even resort to negative behaviors to feel accepted or get attention.

Identifying these students and helping them fit into their peer group through buddy programs or other icebreakers may help improve their well being and feeling of acceptance. Encouraging students to nominate their peers who need such kind of support can help make teachers more aware of students’ social and emotional needs.

What is SIFT: Student Interaction Feedback Tool

Technology has evolved the interactions in the classroom environment and the way students learn and teachers teach. It is easier to communicate, collect, and interpret information, and design ways to use that information for maximum benefit.


The Student Interaction Feedback Tool (SIFT), is designed to empower students, teachers, and administrators with an easy to access online platform to identify, share, and act upon the negative and positive behaviors of other students that they observe and experience in school on a daily basis. It opens a notification channel between students and school authorities to help identify potential good citizens, students exhibiting bullying behavior, and those who may be struggling in making friends or speaking up.

How SIFT Works

  1. The app can be made available online, and to prevent misuse, can be included as a weekly or monthly routine to be accessed through student login in a secure environment like classroom or library.
  2. Once logged in, students can vote for any number of other students as Positive Influencers or Negative Influences (or those showing bullying behavior), and those around them who need Social Support.
  3. With separate logins for Teachers and Administrators, access to the voting data can be customized and restricted as needed.
  4. At the end of the voting period (end of the month or semester) the Administrator or Principal can turn in all voting data for analysis, by clicking the Snapshot button. Individual student and school level charts are generated for comparison and analysis.
  5. These charts can help the school administration take steps to improve the school environment by requesting specific training programs or mental health support for students who need help. The school can also publicly recognize and appreciate good citizens of the school, who may otherwise go unnoticed.
  6. To encourage students to vote, with each vote, the voter earns some points (can be customized by the administrator). The school can treat these “points” as citizenship currency that students can encash for little freebies and rewards.

Based on my algorithm, Student Individual Behavior Scores ( SIBS ) and trends and School Average Behavior Scores ( SABS ) and trends can be generated. These can help track, predict, and compare student level, school, district, and even state level behavior performance.

Statistics

  • School-based anti-bullying programs can reduce bullying by up to 20-25% and victimization by 17-20% (Journal of School Psychology)
  • Schools with anti-bullying programs had 20-30% reduced reported bullying incidents in the first year itself (European Journal of Educational Research)
  • Bullied individuals have a higher chance of having mental health issues later in life (study published in Pediatrics, cited in a research on NCBI)
  • If bullying is addressed on time, children who have been bullied have significantly lower risk of mental health disorders later in life (National Institute of Mental Health)
  • Schools with active anti-bullying policies have more student participation in learning and better overall scores​. (National Center for Education Statistics)
  • Higher scores and better performance in school is also related to a safe and positive school environment according to researches provided by the CDC and Springer.

The SIFT Algorithm

The algorithm analyzes voting patterns, trends, and the relationship between student behaviors and school scores to provide insights. These insights can help predict behavior trends, student well-being, and overall school health. These scores are then plotted on charts which can be useful to school districts, individual schools, and students.

Data Points Used: Student Votes

  • Positive Leaders (students voted as positive influences)
  • Negative Influences (students voted as having a negative impact or showing bullying behaviors)
  • Students Needing Help (students identified as possibly without friends)
